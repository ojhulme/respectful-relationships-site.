Respectful Relationships

Definition

A respectful relationship is one where both people feel safe, valued, and supported. Communication is open, boundaries are honoured, and equality is maintained.

Characteristics

  • Trust and honesty
  • Healthy communication
  • Mutual support and equality
  • Shared decision-making

Disrespectful Relationships

  • Controlling behaviour
  • Dishonesty or secrecy
  • Manipulation or pressure
  • Unequal power dynamics
Impact: Respectful relationships build confidence and wellbeing, while disrespectful ones can lead to emotional harm, anxiety, and isolation.

Forms of Domestic Violence

Physical

Hitting, slapping, pushing, or physical harm.

Sexual

Forcing sexual acts without consent or using coercion.

Emotional

Manipulation, gaslighting, and control that damages self-worth.

Verbal

Insults, threats, yelling, or constant criticism.

Social

Isolation from friends and family, controlling social life.

Consent & Laws (WA)

What is Consent?

Consent is a clear, voluntary, and enthusiastic agreement to participate in an activity. It must be informed, mutual, and can be withdrawn at any time.

Consent & Sex Laws in WA

The legal age of consent in WA is 16. Any sexual activity under this age is unlawful. Consent must be given freely, without force, intimidation, or impairment (e.g., due to drugs or alcohol).

Sexting Laws in WA

Sexting involving anyone under 18 can be considered child exploitation material, even if the individuals consent. Sharing intimate images without consent is a criminal offence under WA law.

Gender Equality & Gendered Violence

Gender Equality

Gender equality means men, women, and all genders have equal access to opportunities, resources, and rights. It challenges stereotypes and promotes fairness in all areas of life.

Gendered Violence

Gendered violence refers to harm caused due to unequal power dynamics between genders, often targeting women and non-binary individuals. It includes domestic violence, harassment, and systemic discrimination.
